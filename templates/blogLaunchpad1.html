Initial Thoughts on The Startup Owner's Manual

09/10/19

After reading the first thirty pages of The Startup Owner's Manual, some initial reservations coming into reading the book persisted. I still donâ€™t believe the notion that there is a tried-and-tested method to building successful startups; however, there are some topics and checklists that were discussed that I did take into consideration and I feel are meaningful for a new company.

The initial concepts that I gathered that I found hypercritical are more in the mold of adaptability. Whether it be me as the sole founder and currently the only one working on Truthable, I need to not be rigidly set into developing, or planning roadmaps, or trying to set up the process of incorporation - I need to be able to do everything and at any time that is required. It is this same adaptability that is extended into the product; the direction of which can ebb and flow in whatever way the user may deem and features can go from concept to production and back to concept for whatever reason the features might need to be scaled back or pivoted in a different use case.

Ultimately, sticking to a firm process without taking risks and adapting to the outcome of those decisions are what I believe limits/restricts a founder of a startup to really push the motivation behind the mission and purpose of the company.
